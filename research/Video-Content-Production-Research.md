# 1. VIDEO CONTENT PRODUCTION PIPELINE (STEP BY STEP)

The video production process is divided into five key stages: Planning & Development, Pre-Production, Production, Post-Production, and Distribution & Analytics.

---

## 1.1 PLANNING & DEVELOPMENT (Week 1-2)

**Purpose:** Define the strategic foundation before any creative work begins.

**Key Activities:**
- Define the video's purpose: What business goal does it serve? (brand awareness, lead generation, product education, sales conversion)
- Identify the target audience: Demographics, psychographics, platform preferences
- Determine desired outcome: What action should the viewer take? (sign up, purchase, share, inquire)
- Set budget and timeline constraints
- Choose video type and format (short-form, long-form, animated, live-action, etc.)
- Research competitors' video content for benchmarking
- Define KPIs for success measurement

**Deliverables:**
- Creative brief document
- Budget allocation
- Project timeline
- Stakeholder sign-off

**Timeline:** 5-10 days depending on project complexity

---

## 1.2 PRE-PRODUCTION (Week 2-5)

**Purpose:** Plan every detail before cameras roll or animation begins.

### 1.2.1 Scripting
- Research the topic thoroughly
- Write the first draft of the script
- Include narration/dialogue, visual directions, and on-screen text cues
- Multiple revision rounds (each round adds 1-3 days)
- Final stakeholder approval of script
- **Timeline:** 2-5 days for first draft; 5-10 days with revisions

### 1.2.2 Storyboarding
- Create a visual scene-by-scene blueprint of the video
- Sketch or digitally illustrate key frames
- Indicate camera angles, movements, transitions
- Map dialogue/narration to visual sequences
- Review and approval cycle
- **Timeline:** 3-7 days depending on video type

### 1.2.3 Production Planning
- Location scouting and securing permits
- Casting talent (actors, presenters, voiceover artists)
- Crew assembly (director, camera operator, sound technician, lighting tech)
- Equipment procurement/rental (cameras, lenses, lighting kits, audio gear, teleprompter)
- Shot list creation with detailed scene breakdowns
- Schedule production days
- Wardrobe, props, and set design planning
- **Timeline:** 5-15 days

### 1.2.4 Pre-Production Checklist
- [ ] Creative brief approved
- [ ] Script finalized and approved by all stakeholders
- [ ] Storyboard completed and reviewed
- [ ] Talent/cast confirmed
- [ ] Locations secured with permits
- [ ] Equipment list finalized and sourced
- [ ] Shot list created
- [ ] Production schedule distributed to all team members
- [ ] Contingency plans for weather/location issues
- [ ] Compliance/legal review completed (if applicable)

---

## 1.3 PRODUCTION (Week 4-6)

**Purpose:** Capture all footage, audio, and raw material needed for the final video.

### 1.3.1 Setup Day
- Transport and set up equipment at location
- Lighting setup and testing (key light, fill light, backlight/rim light)
- Audio setup and sound check (lavalier mics, boom mics, room tone recording)
- Camera settings: white balance, exposure, frame rate, resolution
- Teleprompter setup (if using)
- Set dressing and props placement

### 1.3.2 Principal Photography
- Director guides talent through scenes
- Camera operator captures footage per shot list
- Multiple takes of each scene for editing options
- B-roll capture: supplementary footage, detail shots, establishing shots
- On-set review of footage ("dailies review")
- Continuity checking between takes/scenes

### 1.3.3 Audio Recording
- Capture clean dialogue/narration on set
- Record room tone (ambient sound) for each location
- Separate voiceover recording sessions (if needed, often done in post)

### 1.3.4 Production Day Workflow
1. Call time and crew briefing
2. Technical setup (1-2 hours)
3. Scene blocking and rehearsal
4. Principal filming
5. On-set feedback and adjustments
6. B-roll capture
7. Footage logging and metadata tagging
8. Daily wrap and equipment secure
9. Footage backup to multiple drives

### 1.3.5 Equipment Essentials

**Budget-Friendly Setup ($500-$1,500):**
- Entry mirrorless camera (Sony A6400, ~$600 used) or high-end smartphone
- USB microphone ($50-$100) or lavalier mic ($30-$80)
- Standard tripod ($50-$100)
- Basic LED panel light ($100-$150)
- Reflector/diffuser ($20-$40)

**Professional Setup ($5,000-$15,000+):**
- Cinema camera (Sony FX3, Blackmagic Pocket Cinema)
- Professional lenses (24-70mm, 50mm prime)
- 3-point lighting kit (Aputure, Nanlite) - $1,000-$3,000
- Wireless lavalier system (Rode Wireless GO II, DJI Mic)
- Shotgun microphone + boom pole
- Gimbal stabilizer (DJI RS series)
- External monitor
- Professional audio recorder

**Key Principle:** Over 85% of beginners start with smartphones. Good lighting, stable tripod, clean background, and clear audio can make phone footage look more credible than poorly shot footage from an expensive camera. Viewers tolerate imperfect visuals but NOT poor audio.

**Timeline:** 1-7 filming days depending on project type

---

## 1.4 POST-PRODUCTION (Week 5-8+)

**Purpose:** Transform raw footage into a polished, final video product.

### 1.4.1 Footage Organization & Logging (Days 1-2)
- Ingest all footage from cards/drives
- Create organized folder structure with standardized naming: "ProjectName_CutType_V#_YYYYMMDD"
- Log and tag all clips with descriptions, ratings, and metadata
- Sync multi-camera footage
- Sync external audio with video

### 1.4.2 Rough Cut Assembly (Days 3-7)
- Select best takes for each scene
- Assemble footage in chronological/narrative order
- Basic trimming and arrangement
- Establish pacing and flow
- First stakeholder review for structural feedback
- **Timeline:** 3-5 days

### 1.4.3 Fine Cut Editing (Days 8-13)
- Refine transitions between scenes
- Tighten pacing and timing
- Add B-roll to cover jump cuts and illustrate points
- Insert text overlays and lower thirds
- Second stakeholder review
- **Timeline:** 4-6 days

### 1.4.4 Color Correction & Grading (Days 14-16)
- Color correction: Fix white balance, exposure, and match shots for consistency
- Color grading: Apply creative color treatment to establish mood/atmosphere
- Ensure visual consistency across all scenes
- Can be done in parallel with sound design (they don't affect each other)
- **Timeline:** 2-3 days

### 1.4.5 Sound Design & Audio Mix (Days 14-17)
- Dialogue cleanup and noise reduction
- Add sound effects (SFX) and foley
- Select and integrate background music (licensed or royalty-free)
- Ambient sound layering
- Audio mixing: balance levels of dialogue, music, and SFX
- Audio mastering for final loudness standards (LUFS targets per platform)
- **Timeline:** 3-4 days

### 1.4.6 Motion Graphics & Animation (Days 14-18)
- Create animated titles, lower thirds, end cards
- Design branded overlays and transitions
- Animate data visualizations, infographics, icons
- Logo animations and brand elements
- **Timeline:** 3-5 days

### 1.4.7 Subtitles & Captions (Days 18-19)
- Generate transcription (AI-powered via Whisper or similar)
- Review and correct auto-generated captions
- Format for platform requirements (open captions burned in vs. closed captions as SRT files)
- Translate to additional languages if needed
- Style captions (font, size, position, animation)
- **Timeline:** 1-2 days

### 1.4.8 Final Review & Export (Days 19-21)
- Final stakeholder review of complete video
- Address any last revision notes
- Render final video file(s)
- Export in multiple formats for different platforms:
  - YouTube: 1080p or 4K, 16:9, MP4 (H.264 or H.265)
  - TikTok/Reels/Shorts: 1080x1920, 9:16, MP4
  - LinkedIn: 1080p, 16:9 or 1:1
  - Website: Optimized for web delivery
- Create thumbnail image(s)
- Generate SRT/VTT subtitle files
- **Timeline:** 2-3 days

### Post-Production Checklist
- [ ] All footage logged and organized
- [ ] Rough cut reviewed and approved
- [ ] Fine cut reviewed and approved
- [ ] Color grading completed
- [ ] Sound design and mix completed
- [ ] Motion graphics and animations integrated
- [ ] Subtitles/captions generated and reviewed
- [ ] Thumbnail(s) designed and approved
- [ ] Final video exported in all required formats
- [ ] All project files backed up and archived

## 1.5 DISTRIBUTION & PUBLISHING

**Purpose:** Get the video in front of the target audience across all relevant platforms.

### 1.5.1 Platform Optimization
- Write platform-specific titles (SEO-optimized for YouTube, hook-driven for TikTok)
- Create unique descriptions per platform with keywords and CTAs
- Research and add relevant hashtags
- Select categories and tags
- Upload custom thumbnail
- Add end screens and cards (YouTube)
- Set video chapters with timestamps (YouTube)

### 1.5.2 Publishing Schedule
- Determine optimal posting times per platform (AI-powered scheduling tools can find these)
- Schedule uploads using native tools or third-party schedulers
- Stagger releases across platforms or publish simultaneously
- Ensure no competitor watermarks (TikTok watermark suppressed on Instagram, etc.)

### 1.5.3 Cross-Platform Strategy
- Film once, edit for primary platform first (often TikTok for short-form)
- Export clean, watermark-free versions for all other platforms
- Adapt aspect ratios: 16:9 (YouTube), 9:16 (TikTok/Reels/Shorts), 1:1 (LinkedIn/Facebook feed)
- Customize captions and descriptions per platform norms
- Creators who post to 3+ platforms consistently outperform single-platform creators

### 1.5.4 Repurposing Workflow
- Long-form video -> 10-20 short-form clips (using AI tools like OpusClip)
- Video -> Blog post transcription
- Video -> Social media quote cards and graphics
- Video -> Email newsletter content
- Video -> Podcast audio extraction
- Video -> GIFs for social sharing

### 1.5.5 Distribution Tools
- **YouTube Studio:** Upload, scheduling, analytics, community tab
- **TikTok Creator Studio:** Upload, scheduling, analytics
- **Meta Business Suite:** Instagram Reels, Facebook Reels, scheduling
- **Buffer:** Multi-platform scheduling (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube Shorts, Pinterest, X, Threads, Bluesky)
- **Repurpose.io:** Automated cross-posting to YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, Snapchat, Pinterest, LinkedIn, X, Amazon, Twitch, and more
- **ShortSync:** Upload once, publish to TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram, and Facebook simultaneously
- **Circleboom:** Cross-posting with per-platform captions and timing

## 1.6 ANALYTICS & OPTIMIZATION

**Purpose:** Measure performance and iterate for continuous improvement.

### 1.6.1 Key Metrics to Track

**YouTube Analytics:**
- Click-through rate (CTR) on thumbnails
- Average view duration and audience retention
- Impressions and reach
- Traffic sources
- Subscriber conversion rate
- Revenue (if monetized)
- Comments, likes, shares

**TikTok Analytics:**
- Completion rate (most important metric in 2026 - algorithm prioritizes watch-through)
- View count
- Engagement rate (likes, comments, shares, saves / views)
- Follower growth
- Traffic source types
- Audience demographics
- Note: 24-48 hour reporting lag on engagement data

**Instagram Reels Analytics:**
- Plays and reach
- Likes, comments, shares, saves
- Accounts reached
- Follower growth from Reels

### 1.6.2 Optimization Cycle
1. Review performance data weekly
2. Identify top-performing content patterns (hooks, topics, formats, length)
3. A/B test thumbnails and titles
4. Adjust content strategy based on data
5. Iterate on posting schedule and frequency
6. Feed insights back into the next video's planning phase

# 2. TYPES OF MARKETING VIDEOS

---

## 2.1 SHORT-FORM VIDEOS (TikTok, Reels, Shorts)

**Sweet spot in 2026:** 30-60 seconds
**Aspect ratio:** 9:16 (vertical)
**Resolution:** 1080x1920 minimum

### Production Workflow for Short-Form:
1. **Ideation:** Research trending sounds, formats, and hooks on each platform
2. **Scripting:** Write a tight script with a hook in the first 2 seconds
3. **Filming:** Vertical smartphone recording; batch-film 5-10 videos per session
4. **Editing:** Fast mobile editing in CapCut or similar; add captions, effects, transitions
5. **Publishing:** Edit for TikTok first (trend sounds, TikTok effects), then export clean watermark-free versions for YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, and Facebook Reels

### Posting Frequency:
- TikTok and Instagram Reels: 3-5 times per week
- YouTube Shorts: 2-3 times per week
- Fastest-growing creators post 3-7 times per week across platforms

### Key Success Factors:
- Hook attention in the first 2 seconds
- Deliver real value in the next 30 seconds
- Show up consistently week after week
- Use trending audio and formats
- Captions are essential (most users watch without sound)
- Auto-captions boost engagement significantly

---

## 2.2 LONG-FORM VIDEOS (YouTube)

**Typical length:** 8-20 minutes (educational/tutorial), 20-60+ minutes (documentary/deep dive)
**Aspect ratio:** 16:9 (horizontal)
**Resolution:** 1080p minimum, 4K preferred

### Production Workflow:
1. **Ideation & Validation:** Topic research, keyword analysis, competitor review
2. **Scripting:** Detailed outline or full script; AI tools can assist (ChatGPT for ideas/hooks, Claude for detailed outlines and full scripts)
3. **Pre-Production:** Shot list, equipment prep, set up filming environment
4. **Filming:** Many creators batch-film 4-6 videos per month; talking head + B-roll sessions
5. **Editing:** Assembly cut -> rough cut -> fine cut with transitions, B-roll, graphics
6. **Thumbnail Creation:** Design eye-catching thumbnail (faces with expressions increase CTR by 38%)
7. **Upload Optimization:** Title, description with keywords, tags, chapters, end screens, cards
8. **Repurposing:** Extract 10-20 short clips for TikTok/Reels/Shorts; create blog post, social graphics, email content

---

## 2.3 PRODUCT DEMO VIDEOS

**Typical length:** 2-5 minutes
**Purpose:** Show product in action, demonstrate features, highlight benefits

### Production Workflow:
1. Identify key features and use cases to demonstrate
2. Script the walkthrough focusing on benefits, not just features
3. Capture clean product footage (screen recording for software, physical product shots)
4. Record clear narration/voiceover
5. Add callouts, zooms, annotations, and text overlays
6. Include a clear CTA at the end

### Key Consideration:
- 74% of people who watched an explainer video about a product went on to buy it
- Keep it concise and focused on solving the viewer's problem

---

## 2.4 TESTIMONIAL/REVIEW VIDEOS

**Typical length:** 1-3 minutes
**Purpose:** Build social proof and trust through real customer stories

### Production Workflow:
1. Identify satisfied customers willing to appear on camera
2. Prepare interview questions (not a rigid script - authenticity matters)
3. Film in customer's environment or professional setting
4. Capture genuine, unscripted responses
5. Edit to highlight the transformation story (problem -> solution -> result)
6. Add subtle branding, lower thirds, and music

### Key Stats:
- Testimonial and review videos drive the most engagement overall
- 26% of marketers rank testimonials as their #1 video type
- Authenticity is critical - overly polished testimonials feel scripted

---

## 2.5 EXPLAINER/ANIMATED VIDEOS

**Typical length:** 60-90 seconds (short), 2-3 minutes (standard)
**Purpose:** Break down complex concepts into simple, engaging visuals

### Production Workflow:
1. **Discovery & Scriptwriting** (Week 1-2): Understand business goals, target audience; develop compelling script
2. **Storyboarding** (Week 2-3): Create scene-by-scene visual blueprint with sketches or frames
3. **Design & Illustration** (Week 3-4): Design characters, graphics, icons, and visual style
4. **Voiceover Recording** (Week 4): Hire professional voice actor to narrate script
5. **Animation** (Week 4-7): Bring illustrations to life using After Effects, Motion, or similar
6. **Sound Design** (Week 7-8): Add music, sound effects, ambient audio
7. **Review & Revisions** (Week 8-9): Client review, feedback, final adjustments

### Animation Styles:
- 2D vector/flat graphics (modern, clean look)
- Character animation (cartoon characters acting out a story)
- Whiteboard animation (hand-drawn illustrations appearing in real time)
- Motion infographics (animated charts, data visualizations)
- 3D animation (depth-filled, premium look)

### Timeline: 8-9 weeks for a professional explainer video from start to delivery

---

## 2.6 LIVE STREAMING

**Platforms:** YouTube Live, Instagram Live, TikTok Live, LinkedIn Live, Facebook Live
**Purpose:** Real-time engagement, product launches, Q&A sessions, behind-the-scenes

### Setup Workflow:
1. **Planning:** Define topic, format, and run of show
2. **Technical Setup:**
   - OBS Studio (free, used by ~60% of all streamers) or StreamYard
   - Camera setup (webcam minimum, mirrorless camera preferred)
   - Audio setup (USB mic or XLR with interface)
   - Lighting (ring light or 2-point key/fill setup)
   - Stable internet connection (minimum 10 Mbps upload)
3. **OBS Configuration:**
   - Stream in H.264 video + AAC audio
   - CBR (Constant Bitrate) with 2-second keyframe interval
   - 1080p @ 30fps for standard streams
4. **Go Live:** Monitor chat, engage audience, follow run of show
5. **Post-Stream:** Download recording, repurpose into clips and highlights

### Multi-Platform Streaming:
- Use OBS + Restream.io to broadcast to multiple platforms simultaneously
- "Hub-and-spoke" model: OBS produces once; endpoints distribute everywhere

---

## 2.7 UGC (USER GENERATED CONTENT) STYLE VIDEOS

**Typical length:** 15-60 seconds
**Purpose:** Authentic, relatable content that builds trust (4x higher CTR, 50% lower CPC vs. traditional ads)

### Brand Workflow for UGC:
1. **Strategic Planning:**
   - Define campaign goals (social proof, engagement, brand growth)
   - Choose platforms and channels
   - Create content brief for creators
2. **Creator Sourcing:**
   - Source UGC creators through platforms (JoinBrands, Cohley, Billo, Insense)
   - Or identify organic brand mentions on TikTok/Instagram
3. **Content Collection:**
   - Monitor platforms for brand mentions
   - Filter by engagement metrics and creator audience size
   - Send one-click DM requests for usage rights
   - Track response status (sent, approved, declined)
4. **Organization:**
   - Tag videos immediately upon collection (retroactive organization never happens)
   - Categorize by type: unboxings, tutorials, testimonials, day-in-the-life
5. **Performance Measurement:**
   - Track view-through rate, CTR, cost per acquisition at asset level
   - Compare UGC creative vs. branded creative in same ad sets
   - Monitor time on page, add-to-cart rate, revenue per session on pages with embedded UGC

### 2026 UGC Trend: AI UGC
- AI avatars (HeyGen, Synthesia) can now create UGC-style content
- AI "twins" trained on a creator's likeness can generate multiple videos from scripts
- Allows brands to scale UGC production while maintaining consistent on-camera identity
- UGC-based ads see 35% higher watch-through rates than polished brand content

# 3. VIDEO PRODUCTION ROLES

---

## 3.1 CREATIVE/STRATEGY ROLES

### Creative Director / Marketing Strategist
- Defines the overall video strategy and creative vision
- Ensures alignment with brand guidelines and marketing objectives
- Approves creative concepts and final deliverables
- Makes high-level decisions about tone, style, and messaging

### Producer / Project Manager
- Coordinates the entire production from concept to delivery
- Manages budget, timeline, and resources
- Schedules shoots and coordinates logistics
- Acts as the liaison between client/stakeholders and production team
- Manages the SOP and ensures tasks are assigned with due dates

---

## 3.2 PRE-PRODUCTION ROLES

### Scriptwriter / Copywriter
- Researches the topic and target audience
- Crafts the script based on client objectives and key messaging
- Writes engaging dialogue or narration
- Structures the video for logical flow and viewer engagement
- Develops characters, dialogue, actions, setting, and plot
- Creates the blueprint the production and post-production crews follow
- Revises based on stakeholder feedback

### Storyboard Artist
- Translates the script into visual scene-by-scene illustrations
- Indicates camera angles, movements, and transitions
- Creates the visual blueprint that guides the filming and editing process
- May use tools like Boords, Canva, or hand-drawn sketches

---

## 3.3 PRODUCTION ROLES

### Director
- Guides the overall creative vision during filming
- Directs talent performance and camera work
- Makes on-set creative decisions
- Ensures footage matches the storyboard and creative brief

### Videographer / Camera Operator
- Operates the camera and captures all footage
- Sets up shots per the shot list
- Manages camera settings (exposure, focus, frame rate)
- Captures B-roll and supplementary footage

### Lighting Technician
- Designs and sets up lighting for each scene
- Creates professional look through 3-point lighting (key, fill, backlight)
- Adjusts lighting for mood, consistency, and skin tone accuracy

### Sound Technician / Audio Engineer
- Sets up and monitors audio recording equipment
- Manages microphone placement (lavalier, boom, shotgun)
- Monitors audio levels during filming
- Records room tone for post-production use

### Talent / Presenter / On-Camera Host
- Delivers the script or speaks to talking points
- Brings energy and authenticity to the content
- May be an employee, hired actor, or influencer

---

## 3.4 POST-PRODUCTION ROLES

### Video Editor
- Assembles raw footage into a cohesive narrative
- Selects best takes for each scene
- Cuts, arranges, and trims footage for pacing and flow
- Adds transitions, B-roll, and text overlays
- Works through rough cut -> fine cut -> final cut pipeline
- Collaborates with director to realize the creative vision

### Colorist / Color Grader
- Corrects color inconsistencies between shots
- Applies creative color grading to establish mood/atmosphere
- Ensures visual consistency across all scenes
- Typically uses DaVinci Resolve for professional work

### Sound Designer / Audio Mixer
- Cleans up dialogue and reduces noise
- Creates and adds sound effects and foley
- Selects and integrates background music
- Mixes all audio elements (dialogue, music, SFX) to proper levels
- Masters audio for platform-specific loudness standards

### Motion Graphics Designer / Animator
- Creates animated elements: logos, lower thirds, transitions
- Designs infographics and data visualizations
- Creates title sequences and end cards
- Enhances visual appeal with branded animations
- Typically uses After Effects, Motion, or similar tools

### Subtitle / Caption Specialist
- Generates transcription of the video (often AI-assisted via Whisper)
- Reviews and corrects auto-generated captions for accuracy
- Formats and styles captions per platform requirements
- Handles translation for multilingual distribution
- Creates both open (burned-in) and closed (SRT file) captions

---

## 3.5 DISTRIBUTION ROLES

### Thumbnail Designer
- Creates eye-catching thumbnail images for each video
- Designs faces with expressive emotions (increases CTR by 38%)
- Uses large, readable fonts and contrasting colors
- Creates multiple variations for A/B testing
- Standard size: 1280x720px, 16:9 aspect ratio, under 2MB
- Tools: Canva, Photoshop, AI generators (Midjourney, DALL-E)

### Social Media Manager / Distribution Specialist
- Writes platform-specific titles, descriptions, and hashtags
- Schedules uploads at optimal posting times
- Manages cross-platform publishing
- Ensures no competitor watermarks on repurposed content
- Monitors comments and engagement
- Manages content calendar

### Analytics / Performance Manager
- Tracks video performance metrics across platforms
- Analyzes audience retention, CTR, engagement rates
- Creates performance reports
- Identifies trends and patterns in top-performing content
- Recommends optimizations for future videos
- Feeds insights back into the content planning process

---

## 3.6 ROLE CONSOLIDATION IN SMALL TEAMS

In smaller marketing teams or solo creator operations, roles are often consolidated:

| Solo Creator | Small Team (2-3) | Full Team (5-8) |
|---|---|---|
| Does everything | Strategist/scriptwriter + Editor/publisher | Creative director, Scriptwriter, Videographer, Editor, Motion designer, Social media manager, Analytics |
| Uses AI tools heavily to compensate | Split pre/post-production | Each role specialized |
| Batch-produces content | Share filming duties | Dedicated filming crew |
| Relies on templates and automation | One person handles distribution | Separate distribution and analytics |

## 6.4 QUALITY CONTROL CHECKLIST (FINAL VIDEO)

### Visual Quality
- [ ] Resolution matches platform requirements (1080p minimum)
- [ ] Aspect ratio correct for target platform (16:9, 9:16, or 1:1)
- [ ] Color grading is consistent across all scenes
- [ ] No visible editing artifacts or jump cuts (unless intentional)
- [ ] Text is readable on all screen sizes (especially mobile)
- [ ] Brand elements (logo, colors, fonts) are consistent
- [ ] Thumbnail is compelling and matches video content

### Audio Quality
- [ ] Dialogue is clear and free of background noise
- [ ] Music levels don't overpower speech
- [ ] No audio pops, clicks, or distortion
- [ ] Audio levels are consistent throughout
- [ ] Meets platform loudness standards (typically -14 to -16 LUFS)

### Content Quality
- [ ] Hook captures attention within first 2-3 seconds
- [ ] Message is clear and aligned with creative brief
- [ ] Call-to-action is present and clear
- [ ] Pacing is appropriate (not too fast, not too slow)
- [ ] No factual errors or outdated information
- [ ] Compliance/legal review passed (if applicable)

### Technical Requirements
- [ ] File format is correct (MP4/H.264 for most platforms)
- [ ] File size is within platform limits
- [ ] Captions/subtitles are accurate and properly synced
- [ ] End screens and cards function correctly (YouTube)
- [ ] No competitor watermarks present
- [ ] Metadata (title, description, tags) is optimized

## 6.5 BIGGEST TIMELINE RISKS

1. **Slow decision-making** - The single biggest timeline killer; approval can take 2 days to 2 months
2. **Scope creep** - Adding scenes, rewriting scripts mid-production
3. **Talent/location unavailability** - Scheduling conflicts causing delays
4. **Technical issues** - Equipment failure, corrupted footage
5. **Too many revision rounds** - Each round adds 3-4 days
6. **Lack of clear ownership** - Tasks without assigned owners never get done

**Mitigation:** Establish clear approval deadlines, limit revision rounds (typically 2-3), assign all tasks with due dates, and maintain regular status check-ins.
