## 1. MARKETING DEPARTMENT STRUCTURE & ORG CHARTS

### 1.1 Three Core Organization Models

Marketing departments are typically organized in one of three structures:

| Model | Adoption Rate | Best For | Description |
|-------|--------------|----------|-------------|
| **Centralized** | 28% | Teams under 8 people | All marketers report to one leader; simple hierarchy |
| **Pod-Based** | 35% | Account-based marketing | Cross-functional pods aligned to verticals/products |
| **Hybrid** | 37% | Mature B2B teams | Central functions (brand, ops, analytics) with distributed execution pods |

### 1.2 Alternative Organization Methods

Beyond the three core models, teams can also be organized:

- **By Function**: Content team, Paid Media team, Creative team, Analytics team -- each specializing in a discipline
- **By Channel**: Social media team, Email team, SEO team, PPC team -- each owning a distribution channel
- **By Product/Service Line**: Each product has its own embedded marketing team
- **By Customer Segment/Geography**: Teams organized around audience or region
- **Matrix Structure**: Team members report to both functional leads AND project/regional leads (common in global companies)

## 2. ALL ROLES & DETAILED RESPONSIBILITIES

### 2.1 CHIEF MARKETING OFFICER (CMO)

**Reports to**: CEO / Board
**Direct Reports**: VPs, Directors

**Core Responsibilities**:
- Owns overall marketing direction and connects it to business objectives
- Manages total marketing budget (often millions)
- Aligns marketing strategy with executive priorities
- Builds external partnerships and agency relationships
- Makes strategic decisions about company positioning and brand
- Presents marketing performance to board/investors
- Hires and develops marketing leadership team

**Daily/Weekly Tasks**:
- Review high-level marketing dashboards and KPIs
- Meet with direct reports on campaign progress
- Align with CEO/sales leadership on revenue goals
- Approve major campaigns and budgets
- Attend executive leadership meetings
- Review competitive landscape and market trends

**Monthly/Quarterly Tasks**:
- Present marketing ROI to board
- Review and adjust annual marketing plan
- Evaluate team structure and hiring needs
- Assess agency/vendor relationships
- Strategic planning sessions

---

### 2.2 MARKETING DIRECTOR / VP OF MARKETING

**Reports to**: CMO (or CEO in smaller companies)
**Direct Reports**: Marketing Managers, Specialists

**Core Responsibilities**:
- Translates CMO's strategy into execution plans
- Oversees team performance and manages campaign planning
- Ensures every marketing initiative aligns with stated goals
- Manages cross-functional coordination
- Owns marketing calendar and resource allocation

**Daily/Weekly Tasks**:
- Run team standups and check-ins
- Review campaign performance metrics
- Approve creative work and campaign briefs
- Coordinate with sales, product, and customer success
- Manage project timelines and deadlines
- Handle escalations and blockers

### 2.3 MARKETING MANAGER

**Reports to**: Marketing Director / VP
**Direct Reports**: Specialists, Coordinators

**Core Responsibilities**:
- Runs day-to-day marketing operations
- Delegates tasks and monitors progress
- Serves as primary coordination point between marketing and other departments
- Manages campaign execution from brief to delivery
- Tracks budgets at the campaign level

**Daily Tasks**:
- Assign and review tasks across team members
- Monitor campaign performance
- Attend cross-functional meetings
- Resolve bottlenecks and approve deliverables
- Update project management tools
- Communicate priorities and deadlines

**Weekly Tasks**:

**Monthly Tasks**:
- Analyze monthly content performance reports
- Adjust content strategy based on data

- Present content ROI to leadership
- Evaluate freelancer/contractor performance

**Tools Used**: WordPress/CMS, Google Analytics, SEMrush/Ahrefs, Trello/Asana/Monday, Google Docs, Grammarly, Canva

### 2.6 COPYWRITER

**Reports to**: Content Manager, Creative Director, or Marketing Manager

**Core Responsibilities**:
- Writes persuasive, clear copy for all marketing channels
- Researches target audiences, competitors, and market language
- Collaborates with designers, video editors, and strategists
- Maintains brand voice consistency across all materials

**Types of Copy Written**:
- Website copy (landing pages, product pages, about pages)
- Email campaigns (subject lines, body copy, CTAs)
- Ad copy (social media ads, Google Ads, display ads)
- Blog posts and articles
- Video scripts and radio spots
- Brochures, flyers, and print materials
- Social media captions and posts
- Sales pages and funnels
- Product descriptions
- Press releases
- Headlines and taglines
- White papers, case studies, and ebooks

**Daily Tasks (5-6 productive hours)**:
- Research: Competitor analysis, customer language, forums, reviews
- Concepting and outlining new pieces
- Writing and drafting copy
- Revising and editing based on feedback
- Client/team communication and check-ins
- Small administrative tasks (last 1-2 hours of day)

**Copywriting Process**:
1. **Onboarding/Brief Review**: Gather all project information, understand goals and audience
2. **Research**: Study target market, competitors, customer language, pain points (most time-intensive phase)
3. **Outline/Concept**: Structure the piece, identify key messages and hooks
4. **First Draft**: Write the initial version
5. **Internal Review**: Self-edit and refine
6. **Stakeholder Review**: Submit for feedback from manager/client
7. **Revision**: Incorporate feedback
8. **Final Approval**: Get sign-off
9. **Delivery/Publication**: Hand off to design or publish

**Tools Used**: Google Docs, Grammarly, Hemingway Editor, ChatGPT/Claude (research/ideation), project management tools, style guides

### 2.8 VIDEO EDITOR / VIDEO PRODUCER

**Reports to**: Creative Director or Content Manager

**Core Responsibilities**:
- Produces video content for marketing campaigns
- Manages the full video production pipeline
- Ensures video quality aligns with brand standards
- Coordinates with creative team, talent, and stakeholders

**Types of Video Content**:
- Short-form social video (Reels, TikTok, Shorts)
- Product demos and explainer videos
- Customer testimonials and case studies
- Brand storytelling and corporate videos
- Webinar recordings and event recaps
- Tutorial/educational content
- Advertising spots
- Behind-the-scenes content

**Production Pipeline (4 Stages)**:

**Stage 1: Pre-Production**
- Creative brief review and script development
- Storyboarding and shot listing
- Team assembly (director, camera, sound, talent)
- Location scouting and set preparation
- Scheduling and logistics planning
- Equipment and licensing preparation

**Stage 2: Production (Filming)**
- Set up equipment and lighting
- Direct talent and capture footage
- Record audio and ambient sound
- Monitor quality during shooting
- Capture B-roll and supplementary footage

**Stage 3: Post-Production**
- Ingest and organize footage
- Rough cut assembly
- Fine editing, color correction, audio mixing
- Motion graphics and visual effects
- Review cycles: draft cut -> rough cut -> final cut
- Each review stage has clear approvers and deadlines

**Stage 4: Delivery & Distribution**
- Export in multiple formats for different platforms
- Hand off to marketing team for publishing
- Archive project files

**Tools Used**:
- Editing: Adobe Premiere Pro, Final Cut Pro, DaVinci Resolve
- Motion Graphics: Adobe After Effects, Motion
- Audio: Adobe Audition, Audacity
- Collaboration: Frame.io (review/approval), Dropbox, Google Drive
- AI Tools (2026): Runway, Descript, CapCut

**Key Stat**: 91% of businesses now use video in marketing campaigns; video drives 310% more engagement than static media.

### 2.9 SEO SPECIALIST

**Reports to**: Content Manager, Marketing Manager, or Director of Digital

**Core Responsibilities**:
- Improves website visibility in search engines and AI-powered answer engines
- Conducts keyword research and builds topic clusters
- Performs technical site audits
- Optimizes on-page elements
- Builds link authority through off-page strategies
- Tracks rankings and organic performance

**Three Core SEO Areas**:

**Technical SEO (Foundation)**:
- Crawl website for errors (Screaming Frog, Sitebulb)
- Identify and fix indexing issues
- Review Core Web Vitals (page speed, interactivity, visual stability)
- Optimize XML sitemaps
- Fix broken links
- Audit structured data/schema markup
- Validate canonicalization
- Ensure mobile-friendliness
- Coordinate with developers on implementation

**On-Page SEO (Content)**:
- Research keywords using SEMrush/Ahrefs
- Build keyword clusters and topic maps
- Create SEO briefs for content writers
- Optimize H1s, title tags, meta descriptions
- Improve internal linking structures
- Identify thin or outdated content for updates
- Optimize images (alt text, file size)
- Ensure content matches search intent

**Off-Page SEO (Authority)**:
- Backlink opportunity research
- Competitor link gap analysis
- Coordinate outreach campaigns
- Monitor brand mentions
- Track domain authority growth
- Work with Digital PR team on earned media

**Daily Tasks**:
- Check Google Search Console for crawl errors, indexing issues
- Monitor keyword rankings
- Review organic traffic in Google Analytics 4
- Track competitor movements
- Respond to technical alerts

**Weekly Tasks**:
- Run site audits for new technical issues
- Review content performance and identify optimization opportunities
- Coordinate with content team on upcoming pieces
- Analyze backlink profile changes
- Update keyword tracking dashboards

**Monthly Tasks**:
- Comprehensive technical audit
- Monthly performance report (organic sessions, conversions, rankings)
- Content gap analysis
- Competitor SEO analysis
- Forecast growth and set next month's targets
- Build dashboards in Looker Studio

**Tools Used**:
- Research: SEMrush, Ahrefs, Moz
- Technical: Screaming Frog, Sitebulb, Google PageSpeed Insights
- Analytics: GA4, Google Search Console, Looker Studio
- Content: Surfer SEO, Clearscope
- Monitoring: SE Ranking, SERP tracking tools

**Primary Deliverables**: Technical audits, keyword research clusters, content optimization briefs, internal linking maps, competitor analysis, link-building action plans, monthly performance reports.

### 2.10 MEDIA BUYER / PERFORMANCE MARKETER

**Reports to**: Director of Demand Gen or Marketing Manager

**Core Responsibilities**:
- Plans and executes paid advertising campaigns across platforms
- Manages ad budget allocation and pacing
- Optimizes campaigns for ROAS, CPA, and conversion goals
- Creates and tests ad creative, audiences, and bidding strategies
- Analyzes performance data to scale winning campaigns

**Platforms Managed**:
- Meta Ads (Facebook + Instagram)
- Google Ads (Search, Display, Shopping, YouTube)
- TikTok Ads
- LinkedIn Ads
- Microsoft Ads
- Programmatic display (DV360, The Trade Desk)
- Twitter/X Ads
- Pinterest Ads

**Daily Tasks**:
- Review overnight performance metrics across all active campaigns
- Check cost per acquisition (CPA), ROAS, CTR, conversion metrics
- Monitor budget pacing (ensure campaigns are on track without overspending)
- Pause underperforming ads or audiences
- Scale winning creatives and audiences
- Check for anomalies and respond to automated alerts
- Communicate with creative team on new ad needs

**Weekly Tasks**:
- Bid adjustments across campaigns
- Creative rotations (swap in new ad variants)
- Audience refinements and exclusion updates
- Budget reallocation between campaigns/channels
- A/B test analysis and decisions
- Prepare weekly performance reports
- Coordinate with landing page/CRO team

**Monthly Tasks**:
- Comprehensive channel performance review
- Budget planning and forecasting for next month
- Competitive ad analysis (what competitors are running)
- New campaign launches and test proposals
- Present results to marketing leadership
- Review attribution model and conversion tracking

**Budget Management Framework**:
- Tiered budget allocation: protect proven spend from speculative experiments
- Daily pacing monitoring with defined deviation thresholds
- Systematic creative testing with dedicated test budgets
- Performance alerts for sudden drops in ROAS or spikes in CPA

**Performance Review Cadence**:
- **Daily**: Pacing and anomaly detection
- **Weekly**: Optimization decisions (bids, creatives, audiences, budgets)
- **Monthly**: Strategic review, new channel evaluation, budget reallocation
- **Quarterly**: Full channel mix review, annual planning adjustments

**Tools Used**: Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads, TikTok Ads Manager, Google Analytics 4, Looker Studio, Triple Whale, Hyros, Excel/Google Sheets

## 3. TEAM COLLABORATION & WORKFLOW

### 3.1 How Roles Work Together

**Content Creation Flow**:
```
Marketing Strategist (defines goals & audience)
  -> Content Manager (plans editorial calendar)
    -> Copywriter (writes content)
      -> Graphic Designer (creates visuals)
        -> Video Editor (produces video assets)
          -> SEO Specialist (optimizes for search)
            -> Social Media Manager (distributes)
              -> Email Marketer (includes in newsletters)
                -> Marketing Analyst (measures performance)
```

**Campaign Launch Flow**:
```
Marketing Manager creates campaign brief
  -> Brand Manager reviews for brand alignment
    -> Copywriter writes all copy variants
      -> Designer creates all visual assets
        -> Media Buyer sets up ad campaigns
          -> Email Marketer creates email sequences
            -> Social Media Manager schedules social posts
              -> Marketing Analyst sets up tracking
                -> All teams launch simultaneously
                  -> Analyst reports results
                    -> Team optimizes based on data
```

**Key Collaboration Points**:
- Content teams coordinate with SEO specialists on keyword optimization
- Social media managers work with designers on visual assets daily
- Paid media specialists partner with analysts on performance data
- Copywriters coordinate with designers on messaging + visual alignment
- Email marketers work with content team on newsletter content
- Brand manager reviews all external-facing materials
- Marketing ops ensures all systems are connected and data flows properly

### 3.2 Approval Workflows

**Typical Approval Chain**:

| Content Type | Creator | First Review | Final Approval |
|-------------|---------|-------------|----------------|
| Blog posts | Writer | Content Manager/Editor | Marketing Director |
| Social posts | Social Media Manager | Content Manager | Marketing Manager |
| Ad creatives | Designer + Copywriter | Creative Director | Marketing Director |
| Email campaigns | Email Marketer | Content Manager | Marketing Manager |
| Press releases | PR Manager | Marketing Director | CMO / Legal |
| Brand materials | Designer | Brand Manager | CMO |
| Video content | Video Editor | Creative Director | Marketing Director |
| Landing pages | Copywriter + Designer | Marketing Manager | Marketing Director |
| Product launch materials | Product Marketing | Marketing Director | CMO + Product VP |

**Approval Process Steps**:
1. Creator completes first draft
2. Self-review and internal quality check
3. Submit to direct manager for review
4. Feedback and revisions (1-2 rounds typical)
5. Submit for final approval
6. Legal/compliance review (if needed)
7. Approved for publishing/launch

**Key Principles**:
- Predefined roles and handoffs prevent stalling in review cycles
- Maximum 2 revision rounds before escalation
- Clear deadlines for each review stage
- Automated notifications for the next reviewer
- Single source of truth for all assets and feedback

### 3.3 Content Calendar Management

**Structure**:
- **Annual**: Major campaigns, product launches, seasonal events, budget allocation
- **Quarterly**: Campaign themes, content pillars, channel focus areas
- **Monthly**: Specific content pieces, publication dates, responsible team members
- **Weekly**: Day-by-day publishing schedule, task assignments, deadlines

**Content Calendar Includes**:
- Publication date and time
- Content type (blog, social, email, video, etc.)
- Topic/title
- Target audience/persona
- Channel(s) for distribution
- Assigned creator, reviewer, approver
- Status (draft, in review, approved, published)
- Supporting assets needed (graphics, video, etc.)
- Keywords/SEO targets
- Campaign/goal alignment
- Performance tracking links

**Tools for Content Calendars**: Notion, Asana, Monday.com, Trello, CoSchedule, Airtable, Google Sheets, Sprout Social (social-specific)

### 3.4 Brief Creation & Handoff Process

**Creative Brief Components**:
1. **Project overview**: What is being created and why
2. **Objectives**: Specific goals (awareness, leads, conversions)
3. **Target audience**: Demographics, psychographics, pain points
4. **Key messages**: Core messaging and value propositions
5. **Brand voice/tone**: How it should sound and feel
6. **Deliverables**: Exact formats, sizes, quantities needed
7. **Timeline**: Key milestones, feedback rounds, launch date
8. **Budget**: Available budget for the project
9. **Success metrics**: How success will be measured
10. **References/inspiration**: Examples of desired outcome
11. **Mandatory elements**: Legal disclaimers, brand assets required

**Handoff Process**:
1. **Marketing Brief** (strategy team): Defines the "why" and "what" -- business objectives, target audience, key differentiators, channel strategy
2. **Brief Translation Meeting**: Marketing strategists meet with creative team to discuss brief and clarify questions
3. **Creative Brief** (creative team): Translates marketing brief into creative direction -- visual guidelines, messaging guidelines, specific deliverables
4. **Production**: Creatives execute based on brief
5. **Review Cycles**: Structured feedback at predefined checkpoints
6. **Final Handoff**: Approved assets delivered in all required formats

### 3.6 Meeting Structure

**Attendees**: All marketing team members (or team leads in larger orgs)

**Attendees**: Marketing leadership + team leads
**Agenda**:

2. Campaign post-mortems / learnings (15 min)
3. Review of monthly goals vs. actuals (15 min)
4. Next month's plan and priorities (20 min)
5. Budget review (10 min)
6. Strategic discussion / new initiatives (20 min)
7. Team feedback and process improvement (10 min)

#### Quarterly Marketing Review (Half-day)
**Attendees**: CMO + all directors/VPs + key stakeholders from sales/product
**Agenda**:
1. Quarterly performance vs. goals
2. ROI analysis by channel and campaign
3. Market and competitive landscape update
4. Next quarter strategic priorities
5. Budget reallocation decisions
6. Team structure and hiring review
7. Technology and tool evaluation

#### Daily Standups (15 minutes, optional)
**Used by**: Agile marketing teams, campaign-heavy periods
**Format**: What I did yesterday, what I'm doing today, any blockers

### 4.3 How Vietnamese Small Businesses Structure Marketing

**Typical Team Sizes for Vietnamese SMBs**:

**Micro Business (1-10 employees)**:
- 0-1 dedicated marketing person
- Owner often handles marketing personally
- Heavy reliance on Facebook page and Zalo
- May hire a freelancer for design or content
- Marketing budget: minimal, mostly organic social

**Small Business (10-50 employees)**:
- 1-3 people in marketing
- Common structure:
  - 1 Marketing/Digital Marketing person (handles everything)
  - Sometimes + 1 content creator/social media person
  - Sometimes + 1 part-time designer
- Heavy outsourcing to agencies for specialized work (SEO, paid ads, video)

**Medium Business (50-200 employees)**:
- 3-7 people in marketing
- Common structure:
  - Marketing Manager
  - Social Media Manager (often combined with content)
  - Content Creator / Copywriter
  - Graphic Designer
  - Paid Ads / Performance Marketing (may be outsourced)
  - Sometimes: Video Editor, SEO Specialist

### 4.4 Roles Commonly Combined in Vietnamese SMBs

| Combined Role | Responsibilities Covered |
|--------------|-------------------------|
| **"Marketing staff" (Nhan vien marketing)** | Social media + content + basic design + email + community management |
| **"Content creator"** | Copywriting + social media posting + photography + basic video |
| **"Digital marketing executive"** | Paid ads + SEO + email marketing + analytics |
| **Owner/Founder** | Strategy + branding + key client relationships + budget approval |
| **Graphic designer** | All visual design + video editing + social media visuals |

### 4.5 Common Outsourcing Patterns in Vietnam

Vietnamese SMBs commonly outsource:
- **Video production**: To freelancers or small production houses
- **Paid advertising**: To agencies specializing in Facebook Ads, Google Ads, TikTok Ads
- **SEO**: To agencies (requires ongoing specialized expertise)
- **Website development**: To agencies or freelancers
- **KOL/Influencer campaigns**: To agencies that manage influencer relationships
- **Brand identity/logo design**: To design agencies

**Vietnamese Marketing Agency Landscape**:
- Small agencies typically: 5-10 employees
- Services: SEO, content marketing, Google Ads, Facebook Ads, social media management
- Major agencies: Markdao, On Digitals, Phuong Nam Digital, Prodima, Dinosaur
- Local agencies deliver stronger Vietnamese-language execution and faster iteration

### 4.7 Vietnam-Specific Marketing Considerations

**Cultural Factors**:
- Vietnamese audiences respond to emotional, culturally-rooted messaging rather than aggressive sales tactics
- Emphasis on family, community, and trust themes in marketing
- Authentic storytelling connecting to cultural values performs best
- Livestream commerce is huge (combining entertainment with sales)
- Key Opinion Leaders (KOLs) and micro-influencers with engaged followings drive significant results

**Regulatory Environment (2026)**:
- Decree 356/2025/ND-CP (effective January 1, 2026): Personal data protection
- Decree 342/2025/ND-CP (effective February 15, 2026): Online advertising format requirements, provisions for preventing illegal online ads

**Budget Considerations by Sector**:
- E-commerce: Focus on direct ROI measurement, performance marketing
- B2B: Patient approach with content marketing focus
- Luxury: Brand-building emphasis with high-quality content production
- CPCs range from a few cents to over $1 depending on industry and targeting

### Roles Most Suitable for AI Augmentation/Automation
Based on this research, the following tasks across roles are most repetitive and rule-based, making them prime candidates for AI automation:

1. **Content Creation**: First drafts of blog posts, social captions, email copy, ad copy
2. **Social Media Scheduling**: Auto-posting, optimal time selection, content recycling
3. **Email Marketing**: Automated sequences, personalization, A/B testing
4. **SEO**: Keyword research, content briefs, technical audit reporting
5. **Analytics/Reporting**: Dashboard generation, anomaly detection, performance summaries
6. **Design**: Template-based social graphics, ad creative variations, resizing
7. **Media Buying**: Bid optimization, budget pacing, anomaly alerts
8. **Community Management**: Initial response drafts, FAQ routing, sentiment analysis
9. **Brief Creation**: Templated brief generation from campaign parameters
10. **Competitive Research**: Automated monitoring of competitor activities

### Roles That Still Require Human Judgment
1. **Strategy & Positioning**: CMO-level decisions, brand positioning, market strategy
2. **Creative Direction**: Original concepts, brand evolution, emotional storytelling
3. **Relationship Management**: Client relationships, influencer partnerships, PR
4. **Crisis Management**: Brand crises, PR issues, sensitive communications
5. **Cultural Sensitivity**: Especially important for Vietnam market (cultural nuances, local humor, community values)
6. **Final Approval**: Quality control, brand consistency, legal compliance
7. **Team Leadership**: People management, coaching, motivation
